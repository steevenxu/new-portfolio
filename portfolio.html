Materials Engineering Research Startup

Research and developed fire proof/resistant fiberglass-based materials (patent-pending) to protect personal / public properties in fire-prone regions.

Aluminum fiberglass. Tested to withstand 600C of sustained heat. This is the base model for some other variations that can resist extreme heat.

Sample research done to test the fire resistance/reflecive index of aluminum-fiberglass, silica-fiberglass, and aluminum-silica-fiberglass mesh.

Heat-exposure reaction from samples. Include air pockets into the mesh for more even heat distribution would be a good experiment.


Controlled burn with aluminum-fiberglass wrapped cabin. Study done by Professor Takahashi. This fire lasted over 4 minutes making direct contact with the structure. Video Here

Aftermath of the controlled burn. Stucture sustained 1,200C of heat, with little burn marks at the corners, but no major damages. The aluminum blanket was not damaged.

We rented a local engineering facility in the names of "college students making an impact" for $20 a month, which gained us access to expensive equipments to conduct research.

This project initially started off as a fire-prevention solution that works with the local government, it evolved into a B2C business due to the elaborate product line-up that were offered. We attended the World Ag Expo (and had the best ribeye sandwich in my life) in 2021 to showcase our products, visited and interviewed several fire departments, government agencies, and natural disaster relief programs across the State to build our business models towards consumer needs.

Stee Deliveryüöö

Third-party products delivery solutions platform

Stee Delivery was created to assist local restaurants with delivery options utilizing existing delivery options like Doorash and UberEats.

Reliable API utilization and informative reports to help buisnesses adapt to the changing market. Each business has shown a considerable level of increase in sales since implmentation.

Informative reports to help restaurants stay on track

Stee Delivery generate ~300 orders per unit monthly, gaining $15k+ of monthly revenue, and 100% of profits are kept by the businesses.


Creating business website templates as a free service to gain online exposure. Each restaurant can choose from a range of templates.

A part of the job is negotiating with DoorDash to fulfill promotion requirements. During Q2, we were able to establish a $5,000 credit towards delivery fees and allowed us to lower customer spendings.

Elaborate API integration with DoorDash portals allows users / store owners to seemlessly communicate between the platforms.


The idea of Stee Delivery was created over dinner talks, where one of my friend who works in the restaurant business, brought up the fact that most local businesses don't want to work with big delivery services like DoorDash because they are unfamiliar with the technology, and oftentimes land unfavorable contracts.

It essentailly works as the stores' delivery manager, where we handle customer transaction, delivery requests, and provide delivery instructions, and stores just have to prepare the packages. We also handle their implementation process as opposed to someone from Doordash, provide commission-free contracts, and occasional promotion campaigns to gain restaurant exposures.

At this stage of development, the entire process is mostly autonomous, with occasional phone calls from stores asking for technical clarifications.
